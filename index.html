About

I am a recent graduate of a Bachelor of Advanced Science with Honours in Astronomy and Astrophysics from Australian National University in Canberra, Australia. Throughout my undergraduate degree, my research has focused broadly on star formation and galaxy evolution, more specifically I have studied HII regions, chemical enrichment in galaxies, interstellar medium physics and galactic outflows and jets. I completed my undergraduate thesis under the supervision of Dr. Kathryn Grasha, Dr. Andrew Battisti and Prof. Lisa Kewley, where I studied the impact of temperature and density inhomogeneities on the oxygen abundance in spatially resolved HII regions, using integral field spectroscopic data.

Outside of astronomy research, I am also passionate about sharing my research through my outreach and improving the underrepresentation of women in STEM through gender diversity research. When not doing astrophysics, you can find me playing piano or watching classical music concerts, travelling, bouldering, or at gym.
