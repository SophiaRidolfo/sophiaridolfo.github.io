About

I am a recent graduate of a Bachelor of Advanced Science with First Class Honours in Astronomy and Astrophysics from Australian National University in Canberra, Australia, although I am originally from Perth, Western Australia. Scientific Interests
My research interests are broadly in extragalactic astronomy and interstellar medium (ISM) physics. In particular, I study the birthplaces of stars, known as HII regions, and how optical emission from these star-forming HII regions can be used to trace the physical conditions of the ISM and the chemical evolution of galaxies. I completed my undergraduate thesis under the supervision of Dr. Kathryn Grasha where I studied the impact of temperature and density inhomogeneities on the oxygen abundance in spatially resolved HII regions, receiving First Class Honours, and also worked as a research assistant for Professor Lisa Kewley and Dr. Kathryn Grasha studying chemical enrichment in galaxies throughout my degree. I have also studied the angular momentum in jets of radio galaxies with the ATCA GLASS Survey, and galactic outflows in high-redshift analog local galaxies with the DUVET survey. I am also interested in gender diversity research, and how this may be used to inform initiatives for improving the underrepresentation of women in STEM. I previously worked as a research assistant for Professor Lisa Kewley investigating the relative proportions of female and male academics in all disciplines at Australian National University, and also led research to investigate the proportions of undergraduate female and male student enrolments in STEM disciplines at Australian National University. While the results of this research are not planned to be published, I am happy to discuss them with anyone interested. Other Interests
Outside of astronomy research, I am also passionate about sharing my research through my outreach and volunteering. When not doing astrophysics, you can find me playing piano or watching classical music concerts, listening to music, fencing, travelling or at gym.

Research Interests

The abundances of heavy elements, synthesised in the hearts of stars and returned to the interstellar medium (ISM), provide a glimpse into the history of a galaxy. I study the emission originating from the birthplaces of these stars - known as star-forming HII regions - which carry the same chemical fingerprint as the gas in which they were formed, allowing us to measure the cumulative chemical enrichment of the ISM and the chemical evolution of galaxies.
