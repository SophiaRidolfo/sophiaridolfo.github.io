About

Hi, I'm Sophia. I am a first-year graduate student in astronomy and astrophysics at Harvard, supervised by Professor Lisa Kewley. My research interests are broadly in extragalactic astronomy, specifically star formation, galaxy evolution and interstellar medium physics. I completed my undergraduate degree at the Australian National University with First Class Honours, and wrote my honours thesis on the impact of temperature and density inhomogeneities on the oxygen abundance in spatially resolved HII regions, supervised by Dr. Kathryn Grasha. Outside of astronomy research, I am also passionate about sharing my research through outreach and volunteering. I have given outreach and public talks for ASTRO-3D ARC Centre of Excellence and the Young Stars outreach program, and also led ANU Astronomy Society as President in 2021 and 2022. When not studying astrophysics, you can find me practicing classical piano or watching music performances, listening to music, fencing, travelling or at gym.

Research Interests

The abundances of heavy elements, synthesised in the hearts of stars and returned to the interstellar medium (ISM), provide a glimpse into the history of a galaxy. I study the emission originating from the birthplaces of these stars - known as star-forming HII regions - which carry the same chemical fingerprint as the gas in which they were formed, allowing us to measure the cumulative chemical enrichment of the ISM and the chemical evolution of galaxies.
